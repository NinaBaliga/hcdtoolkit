Explore existing knowledge

Once you have a specific challenge articulated, you want to explore it as deeply as possible so that you fully understand what is needed, what has already been tried, and how you can best solve it.

Step 1 of 2

Begin by creating two lists. On the first list, write down all of the things you already know about the problem. Your list might include facts about the problem and the people it affects, existing efforts to solve it, related challenges or barriers, or other insights. Once you have your list, go back and review it. Highlight anything that is an assumption – in other words, something you think you know, but don’t know for sure.

Step 2 of 2

Then create a second list of things you want to know about the problem. This list might include key assumptions from your first list, other questions you need answered, or early hypotheses to test. List as many things as you can! When you have finished this list, go back and review it.

Are there things on the list you can easily find information about online? Are there resources you can leverage for certain answers?

Field Tip

The best ideas will come from knowledge of what has already been tried, what has been successful, and what has fallen short. The easiest way to explore this information for apps is to go into the Google Play or iTunes App Store and start browsing. Downloading different apps from different categories can help you learn what apps with similar goals or features may already be developed for EVAW or other causes, and how you can build on them to ensure your solutions adds as much value as possible.

Case Study

VXW Award activist Dysco wanted to develop an app to raise awareness about violence against women in his community. In order to understand what has already been tried, he began exploring existing mobile applications for EVAW. Despite there being a limited number of apps in Khmer, he browsed the iTunes Store and found Radio Khmer and Khmer Library, which helped him understand what successful Khmer apps already existed. Fellow activist Phallin also found useful tools such as Zello, and Viber in the Google Play Store, which gave her a better understanding of what communications solutions have been most successful in other countries. Both activists received a training on mobile app features to discuss and discover new technologies in the app stores.
